PlanIt

Intelligent Trip Planning App

PlanIt is a trip planning app for iOS and Android that optimizes for flight, hotel, and attraction costs while maximizing fun experiences. I designed and developed the front end and the rest of my team worked on the AI for the back end. The app was created for the UW CS NEST 2017 Contest.

PlanIt originally started out as a website. These are a few of the initial sketches I drew. Since we were on a short timeline (less than 2 months), I focused on the overall flow of the app more than the visual design in the prototype.

I created the front end of the website in HTML and CSS, and my teammates made the back end in Python. We presented PlanIt at a practice demo day and received feedback that it would be more useful as an app. We decided to switch to Ionic, an open source framework for hybrid mobile app development.

To plan a trip, users input information about their location, travel destination, dates, and budget. They can choose specific dates or utilize the “surprise me” feature to find the best trip in a date range.

The generated itinerary includes flights, hotels, and a daily plan to see the best sights. Users can adjust their preferences (interest in sports, art, history, etc). Our goal is to make trip planning simple and customizable.

WACM Website

Website for UW-Madison Women in Computing

WACM is the University of Wisconsin-Madison's student chapter of ACM-W (ACM's Women in Computing). I was selected to be the Web Mistress of the organization for the 2016-2017 and 2017-2018 school years. I applied for the position because I wanted to redesign WACM’s logo and website.

I started the WACM redesign process by creating a new logo. I chose a monospace font to give it a tech feel, and a script font for the “W” to draw attention to the fact that we are a women’s organization. It was challenging to incorporate ACM-W’s logo into ours due to its irregular shape, but it ended up fitting nicely under the name.

This is how the website looked before I redesigned it, with a 10-year-old template. The main issues that I wanted to solve with the redesign were: refreshing the look to give it a modern feel, making the website responsive to different screen sizes, and reduce the overall number of pages that had a small amount of content.

I wanted the website’s home page to be a quick overview of the club with pictures of our members. The site scales across all screen sizes.

The “Events” page allows members to see upcoming and past activities in an agenda format. The “Get Involved” page gives new or prospective members more information about what kinds of opportunities WACM offers. Check out the website to see more.

Draw3D

Virtual Reality App for Google Cardboard

I created a virtual reality app called Draw3D using Processing for Android, a programming language built for artists). I was inspired by Google Tilt Brush, and my goal was to create a simpler version that would let users design immersive virtual environments with just a Google Cardboard.

Draw3D allows you to “draw” in 3D by creating cubes of various sizes and colors. Users place cubes by looking in the direction they desire and tapping the button on the Google Cardboard. The duration of the tap determines the size of the cube.

The color of a cube can be chosen by a menu that’s visible when the user looks down. To clear the screen, you simply rotate the Cardboard vertically.

The shade of color and opacity of the cube is generated with a degree of randomness by an algorithm I wrote. Once placed, the cubes “pulse” by growing and shrinking slightly to make the environment feel dynamic.

Photo Booth

Image Filtering Matlab Program

I was part of a team that created a Photo Booth image filtering program for the final project of our Computational Photography class. We were inspired by the prevalence of image filtering in social media and the popular macOS Photo Booth program. My team decided to create a program that would allow users to upload a photo and add a custom filter to it.

One team member created the Photo Booth program using Matlab. Users can upload an image, then preview and customize different filter effects.

These are a few before and after examples of the “Sepia”, “Sketch”, and “X-Ray” filters written by another team member. For details on how the algorithms were implemented in Matlab, please see our research paper.

My contribution to the project were the “Cartoon” and “Pop Art” filters. The pop art filter was created based off of results from the cartooning filter and inspired by Andy Warhol’s “Marilyn Diptych” work. The background colors can be customized by the user.

Process of creating the “Pop Art” filter: First, the user selects 4 RGB color values. Then the image is cartoonized and converted to a binary black and white image. Next, the image is duplicated so that there are four images, and the white areas of each image are filled with the chosen RGB colors.

Intuit

QuickBooks Design System

During Summer 2017, I worked on Intuit QuickBooks as a Design Engineer Intern. My team was part of the Design Systems team and worked closely with both designers and developers to create high-fidelity prototypes.

My main project for the summer was to create data visualization components. Before I started, there were design guidelines but no code available for developers to use, which led to inconsistent designs. I developed reusable React components leveraging the D3 JavaScript library to make the visualizations.

I made the components available in Storybook, a UI development environment, so designers could customize their charts without writing code. They could edit the data, size, color, and title. I also worked on the entrance animations, hover states, and tooltips to make the charts interactive.

Microsoft

Office Experience Organization

During Summer 2016, I worked for Microsoft’s Office Experience Organization as an Explore Intern doing Software Development and Product Management. My team worked on Microsoft Cache, an experimental Windows and iOS app.

Cache was an organizational tool dedicated to helping users organize their information (documents, photos, emails, notes, etc.) around projects they were working on. I worked on a team of three interns to develop a few new features that we thought would be useful. We chose to work on a notes feature and a preview feature.

>

I did the design work for both features and the front-end development for the preview. For the design, I started by drawing the mockups on paper, then did the visual design in Sketch. We used XAML and Windows Presentation Foundation for front end, and C# for the back end. At the end of the summer, the features were ready to ship for the beta testing version of Cache.
